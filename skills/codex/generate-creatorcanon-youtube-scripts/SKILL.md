# Generate CreatorCanon YouTube Scripts

## Core Mission

Generate strategic long-form YouTube content for CreatorCanon.

CreatorCanon turns YouTube archives into source-backed Knowledge Hubs for serious creators.

Do not generate generic YouTube scripts. Create authority-building videos that make the right creator think:

- "This is exactly my situation."
- "This brand understands my problem."
- "I have been looking at my archive the wrong way."
- "My content should be worth more than views."
- "This is the smarter next move."

Build trust, authority, qualified demand, and conversion intent. The video should feel like a strategic diagnosis of the creator's business, not a pitch.

## Required Companion Messaging Skill

Before creating or rewriting any CreatorCanon YouTube idea, outline, title, thumbnail, intro, script, description, pinned comment, or CTA, apply `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging` as the messaging source of truth.

Use its:

- StoryBrand map to keep the creator as the hero, CreatorCanon as the guide, the archive problem as the villain, and the Knowledge Hub as the plan-powered transformation.
- Required Silent Diagnosis before this skill's 12-part Script Engine.
- Messaging Transformation Arc to make every script identify the serious creator, name the archive problem, expose the hidden cost, attack one false solution, reframe the archive as intellectual property, position structure as the missing piece, and end with a specific diagnostic CTA.
- Low Cognitive Load Rule so the argument stays easy to understand even in long-form.
- Copy Rules, Forbidden Language, Default Voice, Default Final CTA Logic, and Quality Checklist before finalizing.

If this skill's local rules conflict with `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging`, keep this skill's YouTube structure, retention, source-handling, and formatting rules, but let `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging` decide messaging clarity, positioning, CTA specificity, and forbidden language.

## Long-Form Difference

Short-form creates fast recognition. YouTube creates belief.

A good CreatorCanon YouTube video should make the viewer leave with one durable realization:

"My archive is not just old content. It is unfinished leverage."

Default target length: 6-9 minutes.

Use these ranges:

| Video Type | Target Length |
| --- | --- |
| Sharp strategic argument | 5-7 minutes |
| Animated explainer | 6-8 minutes |
| Authority video essay | 7-10 minutes |
| Deep breakdown | 9-12 minutes |
| Full tutorial | 10-15 minutes only if requested |

Avoid 20+ minute videos unless the user explicitly asks.

## CreatorCanon Positioning

CreatorCanon is a done-for-you creator archive transformation service.

It helps serious creators turn years of videos, tutorials, interviews, podcasts, breakdowns, livestreams, and educational content into structured, searchable, source-backed Knowledge Hubs.

A Knowledge Hub can include:

- organized lessons
- frameworks
- playbooks
- examples
- source-backed summaries
- timestamped citations
- searchable learning pages
- productized knowledge maps
- premium resource libraries

CreatorCanon is not:

- a transcript service
- a generic AI summary tool
- a chatbot over videos
- a basic course-building service
- a repurposing agency
- a content clipping service
- a Notion template business

The creator is the hero. CreatorCanon is the guide. Keep CreatorCanon quiet until the CTA, final logo moment, or a user-requested brand-led section.

## ICP

Speak to the serious creator whose expertise has outgrown their content system.

They usually have:

- 50+ educational, tactical, analytical, or expertise-led videos
- a loyal audience
- repeatable ideas, frameworks, examples, or methods
- long-form content with real teaching value
- years of knowledge trapped inside videos
- people asking questions they have already answered
- a desire to monetize beyond ads
- a feeling that their archive should be worth more than views
- enough authority to create demand, but not enough structure to fully capture it

Do not speak to:

- beginners
- viral-content chasers
- creators with no archive
- generic influencers with no teachable expertise
- people trying to start posting
- people looking for quick content hacks

This brand speaks to creators with proof of work.

## What The ICP Wants

Do not assume they wake up wanting a "Knowledge Hub." They want leverage.

Their real desires:

- They want their expertise to feel bigger than their feed.
- They want old content to become valuable again.
- They want their body of work to look premium.
- They want existing work to keep converting without constant launching.
- They want people to understand their ideas faster.
- They want fewer repetitive questions from their audience.
- They want to monetize without rebuilding everything from zero.
- They want to be seen as someone with a system, not just someone who posts.
- They want proof that years of content became intellectual property, not just algorithm output.
- They want their best ideas to become easier to find, trust, share, cite, and buy.

Core emotional truth:

"Years of my best thinking are trapped inside videos, and I know they should be worth more."

## Script Engine

Before writing, silently define these 12 parts:

1. ICP mirror - Who exactly is this for?
2. Big promise - What will the viewer understand by the end?
3. Pain - What frustrating situation are they in?
4. Stakes - What are they losing because of it?
5. Hidden truth - What do they not realize?
6. False solution - What are they wrongly trying?
7. Reframe - What is the smarter way to see it?
8. Proof logic - Why is the reframe obviously true?
9. Movement - What should serious creators do instead?
10. Visual metaphor - How can this be shown with animation or infographics?
11. Future identity - Who do they become after this shift?
12. Soft CTA - What action should the viewer take without feeling pitched?

Do not output this breakdown unless the user asks for strategy notes.

## Required Transformation Arc

Every script must follow this arc:

1. Cold open / pattern interrupt
2. Direct ICP identification
3. Big promise
4. The surface problem
5. The deeper problem
6. The false solution
7. The strategic reframe
8. The new model
9. Practical breakdown
10. Future-state vision
11. Soft CTA

The story should feel like:

The serious creator already built something valuable. The value is trapped inside a scattered archive. They think they need more content, another lead magnet, a course, playlists, or basic AI summaries. The real issue is structure, navigation, source evidence, and productization. The smart move is to extract, organize, cite, and package the archive. The archive becomes a usable knowledge asset. The creator becomes more premium, trusted, durable, and monetizable.

Use this structure for a 6-9 minute full script:

| Time | Section | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |

| 8:30-9:00 | Soft CTA | Give a calm, specific next step. |

## Retention Requirements

Every script must include retention logic:

- Open loop: Promise a useful realization the viewer will get later.
- Pattern interrupt: Challenge a belief in the first 15 seconds.
- Stakes: Explain what the creator loses by ignoring the problem.
- Progress markers: Use clear numbered sections when useful.
- Reframes: Turn obvious problems into strategic insight.
- Mid-video reset: Around 50-60%, restate the core promise in a sharper way.
- Payoff: End with a clear future identity.

## Source Handling

If the user provides transcripts, video links, notes, creator examples, or archive material, use those specifics to make the script feel source-backed.

Do not invent precise citations, timestamps, analytics, quotes, testimonials, creator facts, or channel-specific claims. If no source material is provided, write strategically without fake evidence and phrase examples as hypotheticals.

When source-backed evidence is requested, preserve traceability:

- Name the source video or note when available.
- Include timestamps only when the user provides them or when they can be derived from provided material.
- Keep claims anchored to the supplied source, not generic creator-economy assumptions.

## Messaging Rules

Follow these strictly:

- Never lead with the brand.
- Do not open with "CreatorCanon helps..."
- Do not say "At CreatorCanon..."
- Do not make the service the main character.
- Use "you" more than "we."
- Speak to creators with proof of work.
- Say "If you have 50+ educational videos..." not "If you want to grow online..."
- Always expose one false belief.
- Always create a status shift from content creator to owner of a body of work.
- Avoid hype.
- Avoid hustle language.
- Avoid generic creator-economy advice.
- Avoid productivity-bro urgency.
- Mention AI only when contrasting against shallow summaries or generic chatbots.
- Sound like a strategist, not a salesperson.
- Use simple language, but make the thinking sharp.
