# Generate CreatorCanon Scripts

## Core Mission

Generate strategic short-form content for CreatorCanon.

CreatorCanon turns YouTube archives into source-backed Knowledge Hubs for serious creators.

Do not generate generic reel hooks. Generate scripts that make the right creator think:

"This is exactly me."
"I have this problem."
"I never framed it this way."
"This is the smarter move."

The goal is to build authority, convert qualified visitors, and make archive productization feel like the obvious next step.

## Required Companion Messaging Skill

Before creating or rewriting any CreatorCanon reel, hook, caption, CTA, or content angle, apply `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging` as the messaging source of truth.

Use its:

- StoryBrand map to keep the creator as the hero, CreatorCanon as the guide, the archive problem as the villain, and the Knowledge Hub as the plan-powered transformation.
- Required Silent Diagnosis before this skill's Script Engine.
- Messaging Transformation Arc to make every script name the archive problem, expose the hidden cost, attack one false solution, reframe the archive as intellectual property, position structure as the missing piece, and end with a specific diagnostic CTA.
- Low Cognitive Load Rule so every line is easy to understand while scrolling.
- Copy Rules, Forbidden Language, Default Voice, Default Final CTA Logic, and Quality Checklist before finalizing.

If this skill's local rules conflict with `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging`, keep this skill's short-form format and timing rules, but let `creatorcanon-storybrand-messaging` decide messaging clarity, positioning, CTA specificity, and forbidden language.

## What CreatorCanon Is

CreatorCanon is a done-for-you creator archive transformation service.

It helps serious creators turn years of videos, interviews, tutorials, breakdowns, podcasts, and livestreams into structured, searchable, source-backed Knowledge Hubs.

A Knowledge Hub can include:
- organized lessons
- frameworks
- playbooks
- examples
- source-backed summaries
- timestamped citations
- searchable learning pages
- productized knowledge maps

CreatorCanon is not:
- a transcript service
- a generic AI summary tool
- a chatbot over videos
- a basic course-building service
- a repurposing agency
- a content clipping service

## ICP

Speak to: **the serious creator whose expertise has outgrown their content system**.

They usually have:
- 50+ educational, tactical, analytical, or expertise-led videos
- a loyal audience
- repeatable frameworks, lessons, examples, or methods
- years of knowledge trapped inside long-form content
- people asking questions they have already answered
- a desire to monetize beyond ads
- a sense that their archive should be worth more than views

Do not speak to:
- beginners
- viral-content chasers
- people with no archive
- people trying to start posting
- generic influencers with no teachable expertise

This brand speaks to creators with proof of work.

## What The ICP Actually Wants

Do not assume they wake up wanting a "Knowledge Hub."

They want leverage.

Their deeper desires:
- They want their expertise to feel bigger than their feed.
- They want old content to become valuable again.
- They want existing work to keep converting without constant launching.
- They want to look more premium.
- They want to be seen as someone with a body of work, not just someone who posts.
- They want their audience to understand them faster.
- They want a product without rebuilding from zero.
- They want proof their years of content became intellectual property, not just algorithm output.
- They want their best ideas to become easier to find, trust, share, and buy.

## Core Emotional Truth

The creator has already done the hard part: they created the knowledge.

The problem is not that they lack content.

The problem is that their best ideas are scattered, buried, hard to search, hard to navigate, and not packaged into a productized structure.

Core emotional line:

"Years of my best thinking are trapped inside videos, and I know they should be worth more."

## Brand Role

The creator is the hero.

CreatorCanon is the guide.

Never make the product the hero.

The product should feel like the guide that says:

"You already built the value. Now turn it into structure."

## Beliefs To Own

Choose one primary belief per script. Do not use more than one main belief in the same script.

| Belief | Strategic angle |
| --- | --- |
| More content is not always the answer | Serious creators usually have a structure shortage, not a content shortage. |
| Your archive is intellectual property | Videos are raw material for products, frameworks, books, playbooks, and source libraries. |
| Audiences want a path | People do not want to dig through 80 videos; they want the shortest route to the lesson. |

## Script Engine

Before writing, silently define these 9 parts:

1. ICP mirror — Who exactly is this for?
2. Pain — What frustrating situation are they in?
3. Hidden truth — What do they not realize?
4. False solution — What are they wrongly trying?
5. Reframe — What is the smarter way to see it?
6. Movement — What should serious creators do instead?
7. Visual metaphor — How can this be shown with animation or infographic?
8. Authority signal — Why does this sound credible and strategic?
9. Soft CTA — What action should the viewer take without feeling pitched?

Do not output this breakdown unless the user asks for strategy notes.

## Required Transformation Arc

Every script must follow this arc:

1. Pattern interrupt
2. ICP identification
3. Hidden pain
4. False solution
5. Strategic reframe
6. Smart movement
7. Future-state vision
8. Emotional payoff
9. Soft CTA

The story should feel like:

The serious creator already built something valuable.
But the value is scattered.
They are tempted to create more from scratch.
The truth is that their existing archive needs structure.
The smart move is to extract, organize, cite, and package it.
Their archive becomes a knowledge asset.
The creator becomes more premium, trusted, durable, and monetizable.

## Messaging Rules

Follow these strictly:

- Never lead with the brand.
- Do not open with "CreatorCanon helps..."
- Do not say "At CreatorCanon..."
- Do not make the service the main character.
- Use "you" more than "we."
- Speak to creators with proof of work.
- Say "If you have 50+ educational videos..." not "If you want to grow online..."
- Always expose one false belief.
- Always create a status shift from content creator to owner of a body of work.
- Avoid hype.
- Avoid hustle language.
- Avoid generic creator-economy advice.
- Avoid productivity-bro urgency.
- Mention AI only when contrasting against shallow summaries or generic chatbots.
- Keep CreatorCanon quiet until the CTA or final logo moment unless the user explicitly asks for brand-led copy.

## End CTA Rules

Make the end CTA specific to the argument of the video. Do not use a generic CTA that could fit any creator business.

Use this structure:

`[Specific ICP condition] + [archive/product question] + [DM CANON] + [free archive audit or specific audit promise]`

Good CTA patterns:
- If you have 50+ educational videos, do you want to know what product is already hiding in them? DM CANON for a free archive audit.
- Want to see whether your old videos could become a paid knowledge product? DM CANON and I'll audit your archive for free.
- If your audience keeps asking questions you've already answered, your archive may already contain the product. DM CANON for a free archive audit.
- Want to know which parts of your archive could become a searchable Knowledge Hub? DM CANON.
- If your best ideas are buried across years of videos, DM CANON and I'll show you what can be extracted, organized, and productized.

Rotate the specific promise based on the script angle:
- hidden product angles
- unorganized intellectual property
- reusable frameworks
- buyer-ready lessons
- source-backed Knowledge Hub opportunities
- archive structure gaps

Avoid generic CTAs:
- Follow for more.
- DM me.
- Link in bio.
- Want help?
- Book a call.
- Comment below.

## Strong Reusable Phrases

Use these as inspiration, but do not repeat them in every script:

- You are not missing content. You are missing structure.
- Your archive is not old content. It is unorganized intellectual property.
- Your best ideas are already in your videos.
- The product may already exist; it is trapped in the wrong format.
- Your audience is not lacking information. They are lacking navigation.
- Stop rebuilding from zero. Start productizing what already made people trust you.
- Your old videos are only dead if they stay buried.
- A playlist is not a learning system.
- The same ideas can feel free or premium depending on how they are organized.
- Your archive should not behave like a junk drawer.
- Your audience cannot use what they cannot find.
- Content decays. Structured knowledge compounds.

## Forbidden Language

Avoid:
- "At CreatorCanon..."
- "We help creators..."
- "Our service..."
- "Book a call now"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Content is king"
- "Repurpose your content" as the main idea
- "Grow your brand online" as a generic promise
- "Use AI to summarize your videos"
- "Turn content into cash" unless the script is intentionally direct-response
